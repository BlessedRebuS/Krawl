Do Not Track (DNT) Policy

Introduction

This website respects your privacy and honors the Do Not Track (DNT) signal sent by your web browser. When you enable DNT in your browser settings, we commit to not tracking your browsing activity across third-party websites for advertising or analytics purposes.

What We Do Not Track

  • Your browsing history across other websites when DNT is enabled
  • Personal identifiers for cross-site behavioral advertising
  • Data collected via third-party tracking scripts when DNT is detected

What We Still May Collect

Even with DNT enabled, we may still collect:

  • Essential session data for website functionality
  • Aggregated, anonymized site usage statistics
  • Security-related information to prevent fraud and abuse
  • Data necessary to comply with legal obligations

How to Enable DNT

Instructions for enabling Do Not Track in popular browsers:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data → Send a "Do not track" request
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Enhanced Tracking Protection → Send websites a "Do Not Track" signal
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Prevent cross-site tracking (enabled by default)
  • Edge: Settings → Privacy, search, and services → Send "Do Not Track" requests
